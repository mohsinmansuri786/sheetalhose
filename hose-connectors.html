Hose Connectors

Stainless steel hose connectors are connectors made from stainless steel material used to join hoses to other components. Stainless steel is a popular material for hose connectors because it is strong, durable, and corrosion-resistant, making it ideal for use in harsh environments.

Here are some details about stainless steel hose connectors:

  1. Material: Stainless steel hose connectors are made from high-quality stainless steel, which is an alloy of iron, carbon, and other metals such as chromium and nickel. Stainless steel is known for its strength, durability, and resistance to corrosion.
  2. Type of connection: Stainless steel hose connectors can have different types of connections, such as male and female threaded connections, flanged connections, quick-connect couplings, and barbed connections.
  3. Hose size: Stainless steel hose connectors are designed to fit specific hose sizes, which are determined by the inner diameter (ID) and outer diameter (OD) of the hose. Stainless steel hose connectors are typically labeled with the corresponding hose size.
  4. Pressure rating: Stainless steel hose connectors are rated for their maximum working pressure, which is the maximum pressure the connector can handle before it fails.
  5. Seal: Stainless steel hose connectors may require a seal to prevent leaks, which can be provided by an O-ring, gasket, or other sealing material.
  6. Applications: Stainless steel hose connectors are used in a variety of applications, such as in chemical processing, food and beverage production, pharmaceuticals, and other industries where hygienic conditions and corrosion resistance are important.
